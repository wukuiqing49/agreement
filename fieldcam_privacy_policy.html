GeoLens Privacy Policy

This privacy policy explains how GeoLens handles information when you use features such as photo capture, location services, metadata writing, local storage, gallery import, and FTP/FTPS/SFTP uploads.

Applicable product: GeoLens / Open Geo Camera Document version: v1.0 Updated: 2026-05-15

1. Scope

This policy applies when you use GeoLens for the following activities:

  • On-site photo capture and gallery import
  • Location collection and geographic tag writing
  • Writing and editing EXIF, IPTC, XMP and related metadata
  • Local storage, display, and management of photos and records
  • Uploading photos to servers you configure yourself through FTP, FTPS, or SFTP
The current version does not process your information for advertising, profiling, or social distribution.

2. Information We Process

2.1 Information you provide or trigger

  • Photos you capture or import
  • Metadata you fill in or edit, such as title, description, keywords, copyright, source, author, and geographic description
  • Upload server settings such as host, port, username, remote directory, protocol, and related toggles
  • Saved templates, custom fields, and workflow preferences

2.2 Information related to device permissions

  • Camera permission: used for photo capture
  • Location permission: used to obtain your current location and write location-related metadata or generate map overlays when you trigger related features
  • Media or gallery permission: used to select, import, display, and save photos

2.3 Information generated during app use

  • Photo processing records such as capture time, processing state, upload state, retry count, and remote path
  • Necessary upload task state such as connecting, uploading, and failure reasons

3. How We Use Information

We process the above information only to support the core functions of the app, including:

  • Capturing, importing, and displaying photos
  • Writing location, time, and business metadata into photo files
  • Generating searchable and traceable local photo records
  • Uploading photos to the server destination you configured
  • Queuing, retrying, and showing state when uploads fail
  • Helping you review record details, upload state, and processing results in the workspace
Unless you explicitly trigger it or configure it, the app does not automatically upload photos to any server controlled by the app developer.

4. Local Storage and On-Device Processing

GeoLens is designed with local-first processing in mind. Most data is first stored on your own device, including:

  • Captured or imported photo files
  • Written metadata content
  • Local records, template settings, upload settings, and map source settings
  • Upload task state, failure reasons, remote path, and related operational records

These items are typically stored in device storage, the system gallery, or the app's local database to support offline-first workflows, recovery after failure, and task traceability.

5. Photo Uploads and Third-Party Servers

If you enable FTP, FTPS, or SFTP uploads, related photos and metadata may be sent to the server you configure or designate.

  • The server is controlled by you, your organization, or another destination you explicitly specify
  • Uploaded content may include the photo itself and embedded metadata such as location, time, title, keywords, and copyright
  • The app stores necessary upload settings and task state locally to support retries and progress display
Before enabling uploads, please confirm the compliance, security, and access control of the remote server yourself to avoid sending sensitive photos to an untrusted environment.

6. Permission Usage

  • Camera permission: used for on-site capture
  • Location permission: used to obtain coordinates, accuracy, and related location data when you capture, import, or write metadata
  • Media access permission: used to select images from the system gallery, save photos to the system gallery, and display image content
  • Network permission: used to test upload connections, perform FTP/FTPS/SFTP uploads, and access configured map tile resources

If you deny a permission, the related feature may not work, but unrelated features should remain available.

7. Sharing and Disclosure

Except in the following cases, we do not actively sell, rent, or share your personal information for marketing purposes:

  • You explicitly upload photos to a server you selected
  • We are required to provide information under applicable laws or legal procedures
  • It is necessary and lawful to handle relevant information to maintain security or investigate serious faults

The current version does not share your personal information with third parties for built-in advertising, commercial recommendation, or user profiling.

8. Information Security

We try to reduce data risk through product design and technical measures, for example:

  • Prioritizing local capture, metadata writing, and record management
  • Reducing loss risk with task states, failure records, and retry mechanisms
  • Recommending more secure transfer methods such as SFTP where available

However, please understand that no network transfer or local storage mechanism can guarantee absolute security. In particular, when you use third-party FTP, FTPS, or SFTP servers, server-side security is not controlled solely by this app.

9. Your Rights

You can manage and control relevant information based on your actual usage needs, including:

  • Turning off location permission to stop writing location information
  • Deleting local records, templates, upload settings, or photo files
  • Editing, overwriting, or clearing metadata written into photos
  • Disabling auto upload or removing remote server settings

10. Protection of Minors

Without guardian consent, minors should not use advanced features that process real locations, on-site photos, or work records. If a guardian finds that a minor has used such features without authorization, the guardian may request that use be stopped and related data be cleared.

11. Policy Updates

When product features, permission scope, or information handling methods change, we may update this policy. Updated versions may be shown through app pages, release notes, project documentation, or other reasonable notice methods.

12. Contact and Notes

If you have questions about this policy, permission usage, photo uploads, or metadata handling, please contact us through the app release page, project repository, or the contact method announced by your organization.

This document is a project-level privacy policy template suitable for the current GeoLens feature set. If you plan to publish the app to an app store or deliver it to enterprise users, you should review it again with your actual operating entity, contact email, server deployment model, and applicable law before release.
